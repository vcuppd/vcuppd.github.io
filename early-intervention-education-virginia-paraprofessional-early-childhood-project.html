The Virginia Paraprofessional Early Childhood Project


Overview

The Virginia Paraprofessional Early Childhood Project is a personnel preparation project working with Virginia’s Community College System to prepare paraprofessionals to work with young children with disabilities and their families in a variety of early childhood settings. The project is a collaborative effort, with a planning team comprised of project staff, representatives from the Virginia Department of Education, VCCS early childhood development teaching faculty, and parents/family members of children with disabilities. Together with project staff, these collaborators are strengthening content in the existing Associate of Applied Science (AAS) degree in Early Childhood Education offered in Virginia’s community college system. These modifications include the infusion of evidence-based content to provide new knowledge, strategies, and support in order to produce graduates who, in addition to being highly qualified, are well-prepared to work with young children with disabilities in a variety of natural settings.

Funding: Office of Special Education Programs, U.S. Department of Education

Major Activities

  • Support VCCS faculty in building their capacity to translate newly adopted evidence-based curriculum content into assignments that reflect best and promising practices in working with young children who have disabilities
  • Support faculty in their professional development activities to include accessing new information to increase their knowledge in regards to working with children of varying abilities and engaging in communities of practice and/or other professional networking activities
  • Work collaboratively with Practicum sites to increase staff knowledge, comfort and strategies as they include diverse populations of young children into their early childhood settings
