Project HUGS (Help Us Grow Strong) for Infants and Toddlers


Overview

Project HUGS (Help Us Grow Strong) is a free mental health and program consultation service in early childhood settings, located at ChildSavers in Richmond, VA. HUGS provides mental health consultation services to children birth to three years of age who attend child care in designated locations in Central Virginia. HUGS services help families and child care providers promote social, emotional, and behavioral health in young children. Staff members from the Partnership for People with Disabilities conduct program evaluation for the project and serve on the HUGS Steering Committee.

HUGS is a collaborative initiative with ChildSavers, Smart Beginnings Greater Richmond, City of Richmond Early Childhood Development Initiative, the Partnership for People with Disabilities, Richmond Behavioral Health Authority, and Infant & Toddler Connection of Virginia.

Major Activities

  • Working with child care providers and families, provide mental health consultation services to assist children who exhibit challenging behaviors or show early signs of social-emotional developmental issues
  • Offer an extensive series of training opportunities for child care providers and local clinicians to enhance their capacity for promoting social-emotional development in infants and toddlers
  • Design and implement program evaluation strategies to monitor accomplishment of project objectives and assess project outcomes

Funding: Project HUGS is supported by the Virginia Department of Social Services CDFA# 92713, Pilot Communities for Infant and Toddler Social, Emotional, and Behavioral Development with funds made available to Virginia from the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act through the U. S. Department of Health and Human Services. The project will end December 31, 2011.
