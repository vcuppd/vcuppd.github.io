Center for Disability Leadership (CDL) --Youth Information, Training and Resources


Website: Center for Disability Leadership

Overview

Individuals with disabilities want to improve their ability to speak for themselves, stand up for one another, and bring about systems change. Unfortunately, many youth and young adults with disabilities continue to rely heavily on other people who determine how they will live, where they will work, and if they will go to college. Research emphasizes that when youth come to believe they can control their lives, they begin to exercise personal control and become active in making decisions that affect their lives.

The Center for Disability Leadership (CDL) provides information, training, and resources to increase self-advocacy leadership skills in youth and young adults with disabilities (ages 13 to 30) who are emerging leaders. We envision that youth and emerging leaders will understand their own power to make choices in their lives; will acquire skills in speaking up and planning for their lives at an earlier age; and some will become leaders in their respective communities and at regional and state levels, speaking up for themselves and others.

The CDL partners with school divisions and local, regional, and state agencies and organizations that involve, or wish to involve, self-advocates in issues important to their lives.

Major Activities

  • FAMILY TO FAMILY HEALTH INFORMATION & EDUCATION CENTER/FAMILY TO FAMILY NETWORK OF VIRGINIA
    CFI assists families by providing one place to call for accurate information, referral to support services, parent-to-parent support, and educational opportunities for learning about healtcare and long term care support.
  • CULTURAL LIASONS
    CFI provides outreach and support to culturally diverse families of children and youth with disabilities and assists parents and family members in understanding and navigating service systems to get the services they need.
  • PARENT INVOLVEMENT ACTIVITIES
    CFI reviews parent involvement needs with Virginia Department of Education's statewide Parent Involvement Network and works with them and providers of parent involvement supports to identify and implement activities to help VA schools improve parent involvement and student outcomes.
  • TRANSITION
    CFI partners with the VA Transition Forum and the Virginia Department of Education's I'm Determined Project to hold an annual Parent Summit for parents of students of transition age. In addition, CFI staff members serve as a representative to the Virginia Inter-Community Transition Council (VITC).
  • PARENT RESOURCE CENTERS (PRC)
    CFI maintains a local PRC website at www.vaprcs.org, provides training and facilitates networking for personnel of VA's PRCs, and encourages new PRCs in Virginia Public School Divisions.
  • LOCAL SPECIAL EDUCATION ADVISORY COMMITTEES
    CFI maintains a local SEAC website at www.vaseacs.org and provides information, resources, training, and technical assistance for local SEACs.
  • COLLABORATIVE STANDARDS-DRIVEN IEPS
    CFI provides the Collaborative IEP Handbook and It's About Me! A Step-By-Step Guide to Creating My IEP in English and Spanish, conducts Collaborative Standards-Driven IEP training with selected IEP teams, and develops training modules for other IEP teams in VA.
