Include Me: Bringing Early Education to Children with Disabilities


Overview

Include Me is an initiative working to increase the capacity of child care centers in the greater Richmond area to provide quality inclusive early childhood education for children with disabilities. Through this project center teachers will receive interventions in the form of training and consultation services throughout the year. The aim of the training and consultation services is to increase their skill and comfort level in providing community-based, inclusive care to young children.

Include Me has been funded by a variety of private funding sources, including private donations and awards from local foundations. To date, funding for Include Me has been secured from The Jackson Foundation, The Cameron Foundation, the CarMax Foundation, the United Way of Greater Richmond and Petersburg, and the John Randolph Foundation. Between 2007-2011, over 30 centers received on-site training and technical assistance related to supporting the inclusion of young children with disabilities in quality early childhood programs. The partnership's Resource Development Board adopted "Include Me" as its inaugural funding priority.

Major Activities

  • Conduct training and consultation for two classrooms in each of 5 centers on quality inclusive child care strategies
  • Evaluate project model through pre and post assessments for teacher attitude, interaction and classroom environment
